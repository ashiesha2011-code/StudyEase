Chapter 1 — Electricity
Electric Charge and Current
Electric charge is the property of matter due to which it experiences a force when placed in an electric field. SI unit is Coulomb (C).

Electric current is the rate of flow of electric charge. If charge Q flows through a conductor in time t, then:
I = Q / t
SI unit of current is Ampere (A). 1 A = 1 C/s. Current is measured by an ammeter, which is always connected in series.
💡 Examiner tip: In 2-mark questions, always state the formula AND the unit. Writing "I = Q/t" without mentioning Ampere loses half a mark in CBSE.
Ohm's Law
At constant temperature, the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across its ends.
V = IR    or    R = V/I
Where R is the resistance of the conductor. Unit: Ohm (Ω). Resistance depends on: nature of material, length (↑ L → ↑ R), area (↑ A → ↓ R), temperature.
⭐ High-value question area: Resistors in series and parallel derivations come for 5 marks almost every year. Make sure you can draw the circuit diagram correctly.
